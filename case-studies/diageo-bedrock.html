One design system, scaled to 120+ brands across 180 markets

Diageo · Global Head of UX & Design DTC, 2018–2025

A global drinks company with well over a hundred brands had a familiar problem at enterprise scale: every brand and every market was rebuilding the same digital basics from scratch — inconsistent, slow, and expensive.

The brief

Build one shared design foundation flexible enough for every brand's identity, but structured enough to stop teams reinventing buttons, forms, and compliance patterns market by market.

The approach

  • Started with a single pilot brand rather than a big-bang rollout, proving the model before asking anyone else to adopt it
  • Built a token-based system — shared rules for colour, spacing, type and components — that let each brand keep its own look while sharing the same underlying structure
  • Grew adoption brand by brand and market by market, refining the system based on where local teams pushed back or found gaps
120+brands on one system
180+markets served

Why it matters for a smaller business

Very few local businesses need a 120-brand design system. But the principle underneath it is exactly what SeaGlass Digital applies at a much smaller scale: get the foundations right in one small, provable pilot before rolling anything out further, rather than guessing big and hoping it fits.
