Bringing safe, human-in-the-loop GenAI to 14,000+ bank staff

HSBC · Innovation & AI, 2025–2026

A major regulated bank wanted to bring generative AI into everyday staff workflows — but "everyday" and "regulated bank" don't naturally sit together. Any tool that touches customer data, compliance decisions, or internal risk processes needs a much higher bar than a consumer chatbot.

The brief

Introduce conversational AI tools across a large internal population without creating new compliance risk, without staff blindly trusting outputs they shouldn't, and without a six-month procurement cycle killing all momentum.

The approach

The work leaned on design thinking rather than a straight technology rollout:

  • Ran discovery sessions with frontline and back-office staff to find where AI would genuinely save time versus where it would just add a new tool to ignore
  • Prototyped a human-in-the-loop model — AI drafts, a person always checks and approves before anything reaches a customer or a decision
  • Tested the prototypes with real staff before wide rollout, adjusting based on where people actually got confused or over-trusted the output

Why it matters for a smaller business

Most small and medium businesses will never touch a project this size — but the underlying question is identical to what SeaGlass Digital solves on the high street: where does AI actually save time, where does it introduce risk, and how do you get a team using it properly rather than ignoring it or over-trusting it? The scale changes. The method doesn't.
